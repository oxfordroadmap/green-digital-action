# Data Governance

## Definition
Data governance refers to the administrative and technical processes used to manage, protect, and regulate data within digital systems, ensuring data integrity, security, and compliance with organizational or international standards.

## Key Characteristics
- Establishment of policies and procedures for data management.
- Implementation of technical controls to regulate data access and usage.
- Integration of ethical considerations and responsible development frameworks.
- Reliance on recognized international standards for global consistency.
- Alignment with broader security objectives and digital regulatory mandates.

## Applications
- Supporting the development of responsible artificial intelligence systems.
- Facilitating compliance with global digital policy frameworks such as the [[concepts/global-digital-compact|Global Digital Compact]].
- Ensuring interoperability and data quality across disparate digital infrastructures.
- Managing data lifecycle and protection in complex regulatory environments.

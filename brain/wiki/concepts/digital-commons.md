# Digital commons

## Definition
Digital commons refers to information, data, and software resources that are collectively owned or shared and made available to the public. These resources serve as a foundational layer for open and accessible knowledge in the digital age.

## Key Characteristics
- **Collective Ownership:** Resources are managed or shared by a community or the public rather than being controlled by a single private entity.
- **Accessibility:** Data, software, and knowledge are made available to the public to encourage innovation and reuse.
- **Governance Focus:** Emphasizes policy deliberation to ensure that technology, specifically artificial intelligence, remains a public good.
- **Prevention of Monopolization:** Acts as a counterbalance to the concentration of power within the technology sector.

## Applications
- **AI Development:** Ensuring that AI training data and models are shared to benefit humanity rather than being restricted by private silos.
- **Policy Deliberation:** Serving as a primary agenda item for international bodies, such as the United Nations, to govern emerging technologies for the common good.
- **Digital Cooperation:** Providing a framework for global collaboration on technology infrastructure and knowledge sharing.

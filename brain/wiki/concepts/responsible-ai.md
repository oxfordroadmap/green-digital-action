# Responsible AI

## Definition
Responsible AI refers to an ethical and systematic approach to the research, development, and deployment of artificial intelligence systems. It aims to ensure that AI technologies are designed and used in ways that prioritize safety, transparency, accountability, and the protection of fundamental human rights throughout the technology's lifecycle.

## Key Characteristics
- **Ethical Alignment**: Prioritizes human values, fairness, and non-discrimination.
- **Transparency and Explainability**: Ensures that AI decision-making processes are understandable and auditable.
- **Safety and Security**: Focuses on robust performance and the mitigation of potential harms or biases.
- **Governance Frameworks**: Utilizes structured oversight and compliance with international standards to manage technological risks.
- **Human-Centricity**: Maintains human agency and oversight over autonomous systems.

## Applications
- **Algorithmic Auditing**: Assessing models for bias and fairness before widespread deployment.
- **Policy Making**: Creating organizational or national guidelines for the ethical adoption of AI.
- **Standardization**: Implementing technical specifications to align AI development with global safety norms.
- **Compliance Monitoring**: Ensuring AI applications adhere to legal frameworks like the [[concepts/global-digital-compact|Global Digital Compact]].

## Description
Responsible AI serves as a critical framework for ensuring that the advancement of artificial intelligence remains human-centric, mitigating risks such as data bias, privacy erosion, and algorithmic opacity. Beyond internal corporate governance, the UN system formalizes this approach through the "Principles for the Ethical Use of Artificial Intelligence," which are rooted in UNESCO’s global recommendations to ensure technology does not exacerbate societal inequalities. By integrating these ethical pillars, organizations aim to support global objectives like the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) while maintaining central human oversight. This holistic approach ensures that as AI systems grow in sophistication, their operational impact remains aligned with global safety standards and non-discrimination.

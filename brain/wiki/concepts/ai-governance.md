# AI Governance

## Definition
AI governance refers to the normative and operational frameworks developed to manage the development, deployment, and lifecycle of artificial intelligence systems. It involves leveraging existing international instruments, such as the UN Charter and human rights laws, to establish a values-based foundation for technological advancement.

## Key Characteristics
- **Institutional Accountability**: Establishing clear responsibilities for entities involved in the AI lifecycle.
- **Values-based Frameworks**: Anchoring technical development in existing international legal standards and human rights.
- **Multi-stakeholder Cooperation**: Engaging diverse actors to ensure consensus and equitable outcomes.
- **Transparency**: Implementing mechanisms for decision-making and oversight of AI systems.

## Applications
- Development of system-wide policy frameworks for international organizations.
- Coordination of ethical guidelines for the deployment of generative and predictive AI technologies.
- Strategic planning for mitigating risks associated with emerging technologies while maximizing their societal benefits.

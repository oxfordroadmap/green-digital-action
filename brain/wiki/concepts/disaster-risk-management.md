# Disaster risk management

## Definition
Disaster risk management is a strategic approach focused on minimizing the adverse impacts of environmental hazards on communities by systematically reducing exposure and vulnerability to extreme events.

## Key Characteristics
- Strategic planning and implementation to mitigate the effects of natural and climatic hazards.
- Integration with [[concepts/climate-change-adaptation|Climate change adaptation]] to bolster community resilience.
- Utilizes [[concepts/information-and-communication-technology|Information and communication technology]] for predictive modeling, real-time monitoring, and emergency response.
- Emphasis on the protection of both physical and social infrastructure.

## Applications
- Development of early warning systems to predict extreme weather events.
- Assessing and mapping vulnerability levels in high-risk geographic zones.
- Enhancing the resilience of critical communication networks to remain operational during disasters.
- Coordinating multi-sectoral responses to mitigate the socioeconomic impacts of climate-related disruptions.

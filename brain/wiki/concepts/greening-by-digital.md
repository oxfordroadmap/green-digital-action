# Greening by digital

## Definition
Greening by digital is a method focused on utilizing digital technologies to enable, monitor, and optimize sustainability improvements across various sectors of the economy. It leverages the transformative potential of digital tools to reduce environmental footprints and support global climate targets.

## Key Characteristics
- **Enabling Effect**: Digital tools act as catalysts for efficiency gains in non-digital industries.
- **Data-Driven Optimization**: Utilizes real-time data to improve resource management and reduce waste.
- **Cross-Sectoral Integration**: Applicable across diverse fields including energy, logistics, agriculture, and industrial manufacturing.
- **Climate Impact**: Directly contributes to the reduction of industrial emissions and the attainment of sustainability goals.

## Applications
- **Smart Energy Grids**: Using AI and sensors to balance loads and integrate renewable energy sources efficiently.
- **Logistics Optimization**: Applying data analytics to reduce transport emissions through route optimization and supply chain transparency.
- **Precision Agriculture**: Using IoT and satellite imagery to minimize water and fertilizer usage.
- **Industrial Emission Reduction**: Real-time monitoring and automation to lower the carbon footprint of heavy manufacturing processes.

# Sustainable Development Goals

## Definition
The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a collection of 17 interlinked global goals established by the United Nations, serving as a comprehensive blueprint for achieving peace, prosperity, and sustainability for people and the planet.

## Key Characteristics
- **Universal Framework**: Serves as a global benchmark for measuring progress in international development and cooperation.
- **Interconnectedness**: The 17 goals are designed to be mutually reinforcing, where progress in one area influences others.
- **Technical Enabler**: International standards and digital cooperation are viewed as foundational drivers for accelerating the achievement of these goals.
- **Digital Equity**: Focuses on closing digital divides to ensure inclusive global development.

## Applications
- **Policy Benchmarking**: Used by organizations and governments to align national strategies with international development targets.
- **Digital Cooperation**: Serving as a strategic guide for initiatives like the [[concepts/global-digital-compact|Global Digital Compact]].
- **Standards Alignment**: Utilized by standards development organizations to measure the societal impact of technical specifications and infrastructure deployment.

# Power Usage Effectiveness

## Definition
Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) is a key metric used to measure the energy efficiency of data centers. It represents the ratio of total energy used by a facility to the energy delivered to the IT equipment.

## Key Characteristics
- Measures the efficiency of data center infrastructure (cooling, power distribution, etc.).
- Calculated as the ratio: Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy.
- Used as a primary benchmark for tracking energy optimization efforts.
- Heavily influenced by cloudification and the adoption of modern, efficient cooling technologies.

## Applications
- Benchmarking the operational energy efficiency of data centers.
- Monitoring progress in facility-level decarbonization strategies.
- Evaluating the impact of infrastructure upgrades (e.g., HVAC improvements, server virtualization) on energy consumption.

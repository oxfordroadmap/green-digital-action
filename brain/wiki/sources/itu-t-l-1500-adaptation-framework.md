# ITU-T L.1500: Framework for adaptation to climate change for the ICT sector - Summary

## Source
- Original file: [[brain/raw/ITU-T L.1500 (Adaptation Framework).md]]
- Ingested: 2026-06-15

## Core Content
Recommendation ITU-T L.1500 serves as a foundational socio-technical framework established by the [[entities/international-telecommunication-union|international-telecommunication-union]] to integrate [[concepts/information-and-communication-technology|ICT]] into global climate change adaptation strategies. The document acts as an umbrella for subsequent standards, addressing the dual nature of the technology sector: as a vulnerable entity requiring physical hardening against extreme weather and as a critical enabler for societal resilience.

By aligning with definitions provided by the [[entities/ipcc|ipcc]] and the [[entities/unfccc|unfccc]], this framework outlines a strategic approach to protect [[concepts/physical-infrastructure|physical infrastructure]] while promoting knowledge-based technologies. It emphasizes that [[concepts/climate-change-adaptation|climate change adaptation]] is essential not only for the sustainability of telecommunications networks but also for the successful achievement of broader development goals, such as the [[concepts/sustainable-development-goals|sustainable-development-goals]] initiatives, by supporting [[concepts/disaster-risk-management|disaster risk management]] at local and national levels.

## Main Points
- Establishes a comprehensive framework for utilizing ICT to address the adverse effects of climate change.
- Distinguishes between the role of technology as a tool for adaptation and the critical necessity of adapting the ICT infrastructure itself to survive environmental stressors.
- Provides a roadmap for future technical Recommendations aimed at countries, cities, and infrastructure providers.
- Defines adaptation as an integration of both material technology and knowledge-based planning.
- Highlights that developing nations are at disproportionate risk, necessitating standardized frameworks to bolster infrastructure resilience against climate-related disasters.

## Core Content
This document provides a comprehensive overview of how the [[entities/united-nations-system-chief-executives-board-for-coordination|United Nations System Chief Executives Board for Coordination (CEB)]] manages the opportunities and risks associated with [[concepts/artificial-intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]]. It outlines the historical context of AI-related policy coordination since 2017, highlighting the role of the [[entities/high-level-committee-on-programmes|High-level Committee on Programmes (HLCP)]] in developing system-wide strategies. 

The source details key governance initiatives, such as the 'Principles for the Ethical Use of Artificial Intelligence in the United Nations System' and the 'United Nations System White Paper on AI Governance.' Furthermore, it explains the internal adoption strategies of AI within the UN, including the establishment of task forces to enhance capacity-building and operational frameworks. By addressing [[concepts/ethics-of-artificial-intelligence|Ethics of Artificial Intelligence]], [[concepts/ai-governance|AI Governance]], and capacity development, the document illustrates the UN's efforts to align technological advancement with its broader mandate of sustainable development, peace, and human rights while ensuring a human-centric, ethical approach to innovation.

## Main Points
- The UN CEB has proactively managed AI as a "frontier issue" since 2017 to align it with the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.
- Governance of AI in the UN is based on the UN Charter and international human rights frameworks.
- The [[entities/inter-agency-working-group-on-ai|Inter-agency Working Group on AI]], co-led by the [[entities/international-telecommunication-union|ITU]] and [[entities/unesco|UNESCO]], was essential for developing unified ethical principles and white papers.
- The UN is transitioning toward internal AI adoption, supported by a newly established Task Force under the [[entities/high-level-committee-on-management|HLCM]] to develop operational frameworks.
- Capacity building, particularly for developing countries, is a core pillar of the UN's strategic AI roadmap.

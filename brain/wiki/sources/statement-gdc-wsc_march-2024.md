# Statement from World Standards Cooperation (WSC) on the Global Digital Compact - Summary

## Source
- Original file: [[brain/raw/Statement-GDC-WSC_March-2024.md]]
- Ingested: 2026-06-15

## Core Content
This statement, issued in March 2024 by the [[entities/world-standards-cooperation|World Standards Cooperation]], outlines the essential role of international technical standards in the successful implementation of the [[concepts/global-digital-compact|Global Digital Compact]]. The WSC—composed of the [[entities/international-electrotechnical-commission|International Electrotechnical Commission]], the [[entities/international-organization-for-standardization|International Organization for Standardization]], and the [[entities/international-telecommunication-union|International Telecommunication Union]]—argues that consensus-based standards are foundational to effective [[concepts/digital-cooperation|Digital Cooperation]].

The document asserts that as emerging technologies like artificial intelligence evolve, technical frameworks must be integrated into policy initiatives, including the [[entities/world-summit-on-the-information-society|World Summit on the Information Society]] (WSIS+20) review. By providing platforms for [[concepts/interoperability|interoperability]] and inclusive participation, the WSC aims to support [[concepts/sustainable-development-goals|Sustainable Development Goals]], ensure the safe development of [[concepts/responsible-ai|Responsible AI]], and provide tools for robust [[concepts/data-governance|Data Governance]]. The WSC highlights that standards transform high-level policy commitments into globally consistent and secure digital outcomes.

## Main Points
- International standards are essential for the effective implementation and operationalization of the [[concepts/global-digital-compact|Global Digital Compact]].
- The [[entities/world-standards-cooperation|World Standards Cooperation]] provides a neutral, multi-stakeholder platform for developing consensus-based technical standards.
- Standards facilitate global innovation, ensure technical [[concepts/interoperability|Interoperability]], and promote inclusive access to digital technologies.
- Technical standards must be aligned with the outcomes of the [[entities/world-summit-on-the-information-society|World Summit on the Information Society]] review process to remain relevant.
- International standards serve as critical tools for monitoring progress and enforcing governance regarding [[concepts/responsible-ai|Responsible AI]] and [[concepts/data-governance|Data Governance]].
